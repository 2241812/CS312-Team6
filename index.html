Children Reached by Provinces

Rights of the Future continues to expand its reach throughout the Philippines.

  • 5,120 Benguet
  • 8,192 Aurora
  • 2,048 La Union
  • 4,096 Abra
  • 1,024 Pangasinan

Rights of the Future began in Baguio City, where a group of dedicated students from Saint Louis University recognized the urgent need to protect and empower children. What started as a small community project quickly grew into a movement, reaching thousands of children across different provinces in Northern Luzon, and continuing to expand nationwide.

  • Learn More

Our Mission

To safeguard, nurture, and uplift every child in the Philippines by ensuring they are protected from harm, empowered with education, and supported in their growth—physically, emotionally, socially, and ethically.

Our Vision

A future where every child—regardless of circumstance—is safe, heard, valued, and equipped to shape their own destiny. We envision vibrant communities across the country where children have full access to their rights.

Our Core Values

  • Child-Centeredness

    Every decision begins with the child’s best interests.

  • Inclusivity & Equity

    Breaking barriers so every child has equal opportunities.

  • Empowerment & Participation

    Children are not just beneficiaries; they are partners in change.

  • Holistic Growth

    Supporting education, health, safety, and emotional well-being together.

  • Integrity & Transparency

    Accountable use of resources and open reporting of results.

  • Collaboration

    Partnering with communities, schools, and governments for lasting impact.

Success Stories

Our work is already creating meaningful change in communities across Northern Luzon.

  • Abra

    500 children improved literacy through our “Reading & Play” program in 6 months.

  • Aurora

    Community advocacy secured safer school routes and shaded spaces for play.

  • Pangasinan

    Mentorship program helped several students secure scholarships for university and vocational training.

Challenges We Face

Our mission is not without its hurdles. Geographical isolation, limited resources, socio-cultural barriers, and underfunding remain challenges. Yet, through innovation and partnerships, we continue to push forward:

  • Mobile Outreach

    Bringing learning and child protection services to remote areas.

  • Community Workshops

    Raising awareness and building capacity among families and local leaders.

  • Technology for Inclusion

    Leveraging digital tools to connect children with resources and mentors.
